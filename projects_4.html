Financial Analysis - A food delivery dataset

Financial analysis of an E-commerce company with a missing business model and without knowing any best practices. Exploratory analysis done in Google Sheets, incl. line item mapping based on IFRS related assumptions. Finishing with a gross profit calculation, company health analysis, explanation profit decreasing critical errors in software and recommending operational improvements.

PART fi


Generate a Profit and Loss (P&L) report for management, up to the Gross Profit line. Make sure to exhibit your calculations and specify any assumptions you rely on. Not all provided facts may pertain to the P&L.


The attached database is a Monthly Report on Deliveries in Month X (in cents) - Comprehensive records of all deliveries made during Month X.


Additional Information:

  1. Customers place orders for food delivery via Foodpanda and pay through a card processor company. Apart from the cost of the food, customers are charged a 5 delivery fee (9 for orders exceeding 100), applicable taxes, and tips.

  2. Restaurants featured on the Foodpanda platform are categorized as partners or non-partners. Foodpanda remunerates partner restaurants on a weekly basis through card transfers, withholding our commission.

  3. In Month X, we acquired 1,000 new customers through referrals. Existing customers can receive a 5 discount on delivery charges for each new customer they refer who subsequently registers through their referral link.

  4. Couriers are responsible for delivering food from restaurants to customers and receive payment (via card transfers) comprising a 5 delivery fee and 100% of the customer's tip for their services.

  5. We incur support costs at a rate of 0.50 per delivery.

  6. We pay our card processor a fee amounting to 1.8% of the order value, plus 0.40 for each delivery.

    PART 2

We are required to issue an invoice to Revolut for 12,000. Several days later in Month X, Revolut remits 9,000 and asserts that they have fulfilled their obligations entirely.

Foodpanda offers First Delivery Discounts for new customers, up to a maximum of 5 per delivery. On average, these new customers placed 1.8 deliveries in Month X. ANALYSIS



Gross Profit is a financial metric that measures the profitability of the company's primary operating activities by subtracting the direct costs from the generated revenue associated with core activities. This metric doesnt consider other operating expenses such as marketing costs, administrative costs or taxes.


In this example we have an approximate gross profit of 46,134.27 EUR having a total of 21,755 line items. Since we dont have historical data nor industry benchmarks, this doesnt tell us how healthy our company is.

We can calculate the gross profit margin which indicates how much profit it makes after accounting for the direct costs associated with doing business. In our case this is 23.62%, which is considered extremely low. This could indicate that the operating efficiency and/or the cost management is dangerously in critical shape. However, we should keep in mind that these are very approximate numbers as two very important things are missing from the calculation: the partngr rgstaurants rgmungrations, the revenue from the campaign dglivgry and the accuratg card procgssing fggs.


If we check the COGS percentage (=COGS/Net sales) we get 76.38%, which means that approximately this much of the Net sales is used to cover the COGS. This indicates that the costs we were able to take into consideration are impacting the profitability, as we cannot consider the gross profit margin healthy.

I believe at this point its clear why we are missing the revenue and the cost side of the weekly remunerations. On the other hand, campaign delivery is a topic I haven't touched yet. In these kinds of campaigns, the companies can offer special promotions, discounts or other incentives to attract new customers and restaurants. While this can increase the deliveries (and can lead to company growth), it may not directly contribute to the gross profit because there's no immediate commission revenue earned. It can happen that the companies sign contracts with the restaurants that the first deliveries are free from commissions. In our case we dont really know whats behind these line items - so for Month X, we can only report the cost side of this campaign.


Also, there was another campaign where the old customers could get a 5 EUR discount on thg dglivgry fgg if they successfully make a referral of a new customer. This is something that is very hard to filter out from the dataset as the Customer Care Team can make various discounts for a lot of orders without the Finance Team knowing the exact reasons. This campaign neither has immediate revenue, but it can

contribute effectively to the brand awareness and customer loyalty that could be an excellent result for a long-term strategy.


For a more comprehensive analysis of the overall financial health, we would need to consider other financial metrics and examine a complete set of financial statements. The Income statement, the Balance sheet and the Cash ow statement could be checked alongside with a breakdown of revenues and expenses for both partner and non-partner restaurants.


The other issue we have that has decreased the gross profit is regarding the discounts and the refunds. We could see (assumption #5 and #9) that in case of a full discount the customers get back the tips as well. Meaning that at the end of the day Foodpanda pays the tips to the couriers in whatever amount the customer chose to give, without getting it compensated from the customer. This must be corrected.

Regarding the full rgfund, there are a few cases when the customer paid a discounted amount on their order, but they received the full order value back. Meaning that the company paid back more to the customers than they originally paid. This is a bug again, that must be corrected.


There were a couple of line items that were incorrect or needed further checking. Its very important to make sure that our processes are correctly taking into consideration every event, even if some of them are so rare that shouldn't exist.


Seeing the current results, I recommend exploring opportunities to improve operational efficiency, optimize costs and to evaluate the effectiveness of the campaigns.
